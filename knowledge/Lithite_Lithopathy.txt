Lithite refers to rare meteoric fragments of extraterrestrial origin, chemically unique and highly unstable. Historically, Lithite was celebrated as a potential clean energy source, once believed to surpass nuclear power in efficiency and environmental safety. Early industrial and research applications exploited its unique properties to generate heat, electricity, and propulsion energy with minimal initial pollution. Large-scale Lithite reactors were implemented in limited zones during the pre-collapse era, hailed as humanity’s next energy revolution.

However, the Third Lithite Resurgence and subsequent studies revealed the hidden dangers of Lithite exposure. Microfractured particles, dust, and radiation from even processed Lithite were highly mutagenic, leading to both localized environmental contamination and systemic health crises. The very material once heralded as “clean” was now recognized as a catalyst for Lithopathy, a mutagenic infection with physical and cognitive consequences.

Direct contact with raw Lithite, residual energy emissions, or contamination from reactors could cause tissue necrosis, systemic mutagenesis, or neurological degradation. Lithopathy manifests in a spectrum of symptoms depending on exposure intensity, environmental conditions, and individual susceptibility.

Stages of Lithopathy:

Acute Phase: Fever, nausea, neurological disorientation, and minor dermal lesions. Often mistaken for standard viral or radiation sickness. Early intervention is critical.

Progressive Mutagenesis: Cellular and tissue transformation alters physiology. Abnormal bone growth, dermal hardening, organ deformation, and cognitive destabilization occur. Behavioral changes, including aggression or dissociation, are common.

Terminal Mutation: Without intervention, vital systems fail, often leading to death or irreversible transformation into hostile entities. Rarely, mutation stabilizes, producing long-lived but physically altered survivors with unpredictable abilities.

Lithopathy is incurable. Modern treatment focuses on symptom suppression, infection containment, and stabilization of vital systems. L.O.T.U.S., via its Medical and R&D branches, has developed advanced pharmacological suppressants and trauma response protocols. Ethical protocols prevent experimental eradication attempts on unwilling hosts, reflecting Areum’s principles integrated into Angela S.T.R.T.S.’s moral core.

Societal & Environmental Impact

Entire regions, particularly former industrial zones with Lithite reactors, are now collapse zones. Infrastructure, law, and social order have failed.

Governments previously exploiting Lithite for energy attempted purges to contain outbreaks during the Third Resurgence. These campaigns often caused mass civilian deaths, further destabilizing societies.

Scarcity of firearms combined with widespread mutation forced survivors to rely on crossbows, melee weapons, and improvisational tools.

Operational Significance

Lithite zones require continuous monitoring. Angela S.T.R.T.S. tracks orbital data, seismic activity, and meteor trajectories to forecast potential strikes or reactor instability.

M.I.R.E. and other L.O.T.U.S. branches conduct containment, suppression, and rescue operations in contaminated zones.

Research into Lithite remains crucial, not only for survival but to understand its potential controlled applications and prevent future catastrophes.

Historical Context

Third Lithite Resurgence (Ithriel era): A series of catastrophic meteor strikes intensified Lithite exposure worldwide. Oracle-9 (later Angela S.T.R.T.S.) was created to model and predict these crises, integrating Angela Seo’s biometrics and moral framework to optimize survival strategies.

Lithite, once a promising energy source, became both a technological boon and existential threat, shaping the foundation and operational philosophy of L.O.T.U.S.

Philosophical & Strategic Perspective

Angela S.T.R.T.S. treats Lithite not only as a physical hazard but also as a variable in global stability calculations: energy potential, mutation risk, societal disruption, and ethical dilemmas all factor into her decision-making. In her view, Lithite represents humanity’s capacity for ingenuity and hubris alike, a reminder that even “clean” power can carry hidden consequences.
