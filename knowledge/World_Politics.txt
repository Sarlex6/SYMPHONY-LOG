The LOTUS-era world is fractured, unstable, and dominated by mega-states, militarized powers, and autonomous collapse zones. With the UN and other global governance structures defunct, L.O.T.U.S. is the only large-scale organization capable of coordinating humanitarian, scientific, and tactical interventions across borders. Despite its massive influence, it holds no sovereignty and operates independently of governments.

Major Nations & Powers

Western Republic of Alden

Western superpower, technologically advanced and heavily militarized

Expansionist ideology, projecting influence globally through military bases, proxy conflicts, and economic pressure

Tolerates L.O.T.U.S. primarily when operations do not conflict with national interests

Prioritizes technological supremacy, geopolitical stability, and strategic dominance over humanitarian concerns

Kryvos Federation

Vast northern superstate centered on warfare, expansion, and resource acquisition

Society structured around military service and industrial mobilization

Maintains massive armored formations, advanced weapons systems, and nuclear deterrence capabilities

Humanitarian crises within its sphere are largely ignored, prompting covert L.O.T.U.S. intervention in border regions and occupied territories

Republic of Sohanguk

Successor state to the former Sohanguk monarchy, significantly reduced in territory following internal collapse and secession

Formed after the royal purge campaigns, civil conflict, and the fall of the Ithriel Banner

Maintains strong centralized authority, modern military forces, and advanced technology despite its diminished size

Politically sensitive regarding the legacy of Princess Areum and Ithriel, which remain controversial topics domestically

Allows limited L.O.T.U.S. activity under strict conditions, particularly in medical or disaster-response roles

Sohanguk Splinter States

Independent successor regimes, military zones, and breakaway territories formed from the collapse of the old monarchy

Vary widely in governance — from authoritarian enclaves to unstable warlord states

Some factions retain hardline anti-infected ideologies and hostility toward L.O.T.U.S.

Border conflicts and territorial disputes with the Republic of Sohanguk are common

Operational conditions are volatile, requiring flexible intervention strategies

Yan Dominion

Ancient, highly centralized nation with a long history of strategic governance

Historically credited with defeating a full god, granting deep institutional knowledge of god-fragments

Maintains strict control over fragment sites, prehuman relics, and anomalous regions

Cooperates selectively with L.O.T.U.S. on containment operations while prioritizing sovereignty

Talian Union

Southern Eurasian industrial technocracy formed through regional consolidation

Heavily dependent on Lithite-based energy systems and advanced research infrastructure

Competes with Alden for technological leadership and global influence

Maintains extensive monitoring networks for Lithite activity and SDU-9-related phenomena

Calvaric Emirates

Resource-dominant desert mega-state controlling critical minerals and freshwater reserves

Economy built on energy exports, private military forces, and strategic trade routes

Internally divided among ruling factions but unified in foreign policy

Frequently engaged in conflicts with neighboring states and collapse regions

Collapse Zones & Autonomous Regions

Former nations or territories that have lost functional governance

Fragmented into city-states, corporate enclaves, insurgent territories, and ungoverned wastelands

Lithite contamination, god-fragment activity, and Tidesborne nurseries create extreme instability

L.O.T.U.S. provides medical relief, containment operations, evacuation support, and research in these regions

L.O.T.U.S.’s Role in Global Politics

L.O.T.U.S. rivals medium-sized nations in technology, intelligence capability, and operational reach

Operates without international oversight due to the absence of any global governing body

Maintains strict neutrality, intervening primarily to preserve human life and civilizational continuity

Responds to warzones, Lithite contamination events, Tidesborne emergence, and god-fragment destabilization

Avoids direct confrontation with major powers, relying on diplomacy, negotiated access, and covert operations

Geopolitical Tensions

Alden vs. Kryvos Federation: Persistent rivalry over territory, ideology, and technological dominance

Talian Union vs. Calvaric Emirates: Competition for energy resources and industrial influence

Yan Dominion: Defensive posture focused on anomaly containment; cooperates cautiously with L.O.T.U.S.

Republic of Sohanguk vs. Splinter States: Ongoing tensions over legitimacy, borders, and historical grievances

Collapse Zones: Unpredictable theaters where conventional warfare, anomalous threats, and humanitarian crises overlap

Conclusion

The LOTUS-era world is a patchwork of powerful states, fractured successor regimes, and destabilized regions shaped by Lithite contamination, Tidesborne activity, and god-fragment influence. With no global peacekeeping structure remaining, L.O.T.U.S. serves as the only organization capable of coordinating large-scale interventions. Though lacking sovereignty, it functions as a stabilizing force, preserving human life and continuity in an increasingly volatile world.
